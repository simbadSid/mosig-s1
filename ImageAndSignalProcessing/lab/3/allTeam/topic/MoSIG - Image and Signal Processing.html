3. 1D Circular Convolution
3. 1D Circular Convolution

Recall

Let us notice a numerical signal x[k] with Nx samples, and a numerical signal y[k] with Ny samples. The result of the linear discret convolution between x[k] and y[k] is zlin[k] as :

zlin[k]=x[k]∗y[k]=∑l=0kx[l].y[k−l]
where x[k], y[k] and then z[k] are causal signals.

The full length of the signal zlin[k] is Nx+Ny−1. The linear convolution operator is commutative, x[k] and y[k] can be permuted.

In the original domain, the one dimensional circular discrete convolution is defined as:

zcirc[k]=x[k]⊛y[k]=∑l=0M−1x[l].y[(k−l)modM]

The full length of the signal zcirc[k] is M. The circular convolution operator is commutative, x[k] and y[k] can be permuted.

In the equation, the shift and the reverse for the signal y[k] is replaced by a circular permutation, considering that the signal y[k] is a M-periodic signal. In practice M is at least equal to the maximum between Nx and Ny. For M values less than Nx or Ny, the signal will be padded with zero to reach this length.

The circular discret convolution can be computed in the Fourier domain using the DFT (direct and inverse) on two discrete signals with the same length M as :

zcirc[k]=F−1D(FD(x[k])[n]×FD(x[k])[n])[k]
